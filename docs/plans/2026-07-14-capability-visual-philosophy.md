# Connected Evidence

Connected Evidence treats scientific organization as a visible transformation from
physical complexity into legible structure. Space is divided into capture, reasoning,
and durable record states; movement across the canvas carries the meaning. Objects are
repeated patiently so scale can be felt before it is explained.

Color acts as a research vocabulary. Cream paper makes the system feel owned and
inspectable, black construction lines provide material honesty, and concentrated mint,
lime, and cyan mark moments when information becomes useful. Pink appears only where
context is unresolved. Every hue is calibrated with painstaking attention so color
communicates before a label is read.

Forms behave like instruments rather than decoration. Cards, grids, branches, and
provenance lines reveal capture, reuse, and connection through geometry. Large gestures
establish the transformation; small clinical marks reward close inspection. The work
should feel meticulously crafted by someone fluent in both laboratory practice and
information design.

Typography is sparse and structural: one declarative promise, a few record identifiers,
and compact field labels. Words anchor what the composition already proves. Alignment,
spacing, and rhythm receive master-level execution, with every object appearing placed
after countless refinements rather than dropped into a template.

The quiet conceptual thread is traceability. A tube, procedure, or idea never becomes
an anonymous digital token; each retains a visible path to where it came from and where
it goes next. The final work should reward both immediate understanding and careful
reading, with the polished authority of a scientific artifact labored over with care.
